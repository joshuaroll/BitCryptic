Ideas for Review — Bit Cryptic World
======================================

8. Better Mystic Peak Puzzles
-----------------------------
Concept: Mystic Peak Observatory currently has placeholder actions (stargaze, star charts).
Replace with a proper puzzle experience themed around constellations and celestial wordplay.

Ideas:
- Star Chart Puzzle: Connect stars to spell out hidden words. The player traces lines
  between stars (nodes) to form letters, like a celestial version of connect-the-dots.
- Constellation Clues: Each constellation has a cryptic clue embedded in its name or shape.
  e.g., "The Great Bear hides a weapon (3)" — answer: ARM (hidden in beARM).
- Telescope Minigame: Looking through the telescope reveals scrambled star names the player
  must unscramble. Could tie into unlocking the Moon location as a reward.
- Difficulty Ramp: Start with simple double-definition clues, escalate to container and
  deletion clues. Mark (from the Moon) could have written some of the harder ones.
- Visual: Dark sky with animated twinkling. Clue text floats among the stars. Solving a
  puzzle causes a constellation to light up permanently.


9. Terminal Easter Eggs — bark, leaf, pine
------------------------------------------
Concept: Entering nature-themed words into the Town Square terminal triggers fun hidden
responses that reward curious players without unlocking anything game-breaking.

Ideas:
- "BARK": Terminal responds with a riddle about the Enigma Forest.
  e.g., "The trees remember. A fox watches from the undergrowth... it barks once, then
  vanishes." Could briefly flash a fox sprite near the forest on the map.
- "LEAF": Terminal responds with a seasonal poem or a cryptic clue.
  e.g., "A leaf falls from the ancient oak. On it, scratched in tiny letters: 'Turn over
  a new one to start fresh (4).' " (answer: LEAF — double definition).
  Could trigger a leaf-falling particle animation over the island.
- "PINE": Terminal responds with a longing message from a character.
  e.g., "A message from the Puzzle Workshop: 'Cryptic Croc misses his old friend Pine
  Martin. If you see him in the forest, say hello.' "
  Could unlock a small Pine Martin NPC hidden in the forest scene.
- General approach: Keep these light and flavourful, not required for progression. They
  reward exploration and make the terminal feel alive. Track them in achievements.
